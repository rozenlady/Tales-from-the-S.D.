Tales from the S.D.

In TFTSD we host biweekly dinners where we combine Shabbat’s traditions with the community building exercises and other aspects of a Japanese Tea Ceremony. We take Shabbat’s break from the mundane, the need to be lively, and the opportunity to be grateful. The community building exercises we incorporate include interactive questioning and guidance that nurture safety so that vulnerability can flourish. This questioning and guidance are themed around gratitude and hope. We include Tea Ceremony traditions that involve the necessity of ritualized aesthetics of the event to instill beauty, humbleness, kindness and respect. Finally, we keep the number of attendees manageable, which guarantees everyone’s participation and keeps people from reverting to their natural approach of interaction.

We promote these biweekly dinners through friends and friends of friends. The name itself forces this word of mouth promotion to be followed by an explanation of its origin. We curate each dinner so that it serves our guests as both a social and professional networking event.
